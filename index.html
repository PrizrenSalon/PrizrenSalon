About the Salon

The Prizren Salon, founded by writer Mikra Namani, brings together regional and international acclaimed writers and poets in Prizren, Kosovo, for a two-day visit which coincides with Dokufest, an international documentary and short film festival. The central focus of the visit is a literary evening organized on the second day, featuring several writers reading from their work — novels, essays, poetry, and more. Last year's edition welcomed writers, novelists, poets, translators, and critics, including Christian Lorentzen (NYC), Ryan Ruby (Berlin), Josh Furst (NYC), Suzana Vuljevic (Chicago), and Kosovar poet Ervina Halili.

The 2026 readers will be announced in the coming weeks while the reading is scheduled to take place on Aug 5, at 6PM at the iconic BarAca venue in old town Prizren.

Anyone interested in the reading and meeting the Salon writers is welcome to join us. Please use the form on this website to register so that we can get an accurate count of guests.

Dinner at Hani i Vjeter

(Only for Organizers and Readers)

Old town, Prizren · beside the Stone Bridge on the Bistrica river

From the bazaar, walk south along Sheshi Shatërvani square and follow the river path — the inn's wooden terrace overhangs the water. A five-minute walk from the League of Prizren.

Hani i Vjeter ("The Old Inn") serves traditional Albanian and Ottoman-influenced cuisine: slow-roasted lamb, flija (layered crêpe pie), tave kosi (baked yoghurt and lamb), fresh flatbreads baked in the oven, and local Sharr mountain cheeses.

Venue

Prizren,
Kosovo

Prizren is one of the oldest cities in the Western Balkans, home to a remarkably preserved Ottoman old town, a medieval fortress, and a culture of extraordinary plurality. The city has always been a crossroads of languages.
